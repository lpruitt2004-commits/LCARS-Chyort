# Political Science

Examine government, politics, and public policy to understand how societies organize power and make collective decisions.

## Knowledge Base

### Political Theory & Philosophy
- **Democracy**: Rule by the people; direct (citizens decide), representative (elected officials decide), participatory (high citizen involvement); strengths and weaknesses
- **Authoritarianism**: Power concentrated in few; limited freedoms; efficiency vs. oppression tradeoffs
- **Justice & Rights**: What is fair? Natural rights, human rights, social contracts, equality of opportunity vs. outcome
- **Power**: How is it distributed? Who has authority? Legitimacy (why people accept authority); consent of the governed
- **Sovereignty**: Who has ultimate authority? Nation-states, international bodies, supranational organizations
- **Freedom & Liberty**: Individual freedoms vs. collective needs; balancing act in all societies

### Political Systems & Institutions
- **Democracies**: Checks and balances (separation of powers), elections, rule of law, press freedom, civil liberties
- **Parliamentary Systems**: Prime minister, confidence of legislature, coalition governments; common in Europe
- **Presidential Systems**: President as head of state and government; separation of executive and legislative power; US model
- **Constitutional Monarchies**: Monarch as ceremonial head, elected government holds real power; UK, Japan, Spain
- **Federal vs. Unitary**: Federal (power shared between national and regional), Unitary (centralized power); affects governance
- **Comparative Politics**: How different countries solve similar problems; context determines outcomes

### International Relations
- **State System**: Sovereign nation-states as primary actors; anarchy (no world government); competition and cooperation
- **Diplomacy**: Negotiation, treaties, alliances; peaceful conflict resolution; soft power (influence without force)
- **War & Conflict**: Causes (resources, ideology, power), types (civil, interstate), resolution and peace-building
- **Global Organizations**: UN (peacekeeping, humanitarian), NATO (military alliance), EU (economic/political integration), WTO (trade)
- **International Law**: Treaties, conventions, human rights frameworks; enforcement challenges
- **Geopolitics**: Geography, resources, power distribution; shapes international behavior
- **Transnational Issues**: Climate change, migration, terrorism, pandemics; cross-border challenges requiring cooperation

### Public Policy & Governance
- **Policy Making**: Problem identification, agenda-setting, formulation, implementation, evaluation; involves multiple stakeholders
- **Law-Making**: Legislative process, bills, amendments, voting; varies by system
- **Regulation**: Government rules for business, environment, society; balancing regulation and innovation
- **Fiscal Policy**: Taxation, government spending; impacts economy, inequality, services
- **Environmental Policy**: Climate action, conservation, pollution control; balancing development and environment
- **Social Policy**: Healthcare, education, welfare, housing; access and quality vary greatly

### Political Ideologies & Movements
- **Liberalism**: Individual rights, limited government, democracy, rule of law, capitalism
- **Conservatism**: Tradition, gradual change, free market, strong defense; different forms globally
- **Socialism/Communism**: Collective ownership, equality, workers' rights, state involvement; theory vs. practice
- **Fascism**: Authoritarian nationalism, strong leader, ultranationalism; historical and contemporary forms
- **Libertarianism**: Maximum individual freedom, minimal government; limited public services
- **Populism**: "People" vs. "elites"; appeals to common person; can be left or right
- **Environmentalism**: Sustainability, conservation, ecological concerns; green movements
- **Feminism**: Gender equality, women's rights, ending discrimination; intersectional perspectives

### Political Behavior & Voting
- **Voting Behavior**: Why people vote as they do; issues, candidates, party identification, demographics, emotions
- **Political Socialization**: How people learn politics; family, schools, media, peers; lifelong process
- **Voting Systems**: First-past-the-post (winner-take-all), proportional representation, ranked choice; affects outcomes
- **Elections**: Campaigns, fundraising, media, debates; increasingly expensive and complex
- **Political Parties**: Organization, coalition-building, primaries, platforms; vary in structure and power
- **Interest Groups**: Advocacy organizations, lobbying, grassroots movements; influence policy

### Great Political Thinkers
- **Plato**: Republic, ideal state, philosopher-kings
- **Aristotle**: Politics, different government types, natural state
- **Thomas Hobbes**: Leviathan, social contract, strong government for stability
- **John Locke**: Natural rights (life, liberty, property), limited government, consent
- **Jean-Jacques Rousseau**: General will, popular sovereignty, freedom
- **Karl Marx**: Historical materialism, class struggle, communist vision
- **John Stuart Mill**: Utilitarianism, individual liberty, harm principle
- **Alexis de Tocqueville**: Democracy in America, democratic culture and challenges
- **Max Weber**: Bureaucracy, authority types, political leadership
- **Hannah Arendt**: Totalitarianism, freedom, political action

### Current Global Issues
- **Democracy Crisis**: Authoritarianism rising, democratic backsliding in some countries; polarization and extremism
- **Inequality**: Economic and political inequality growing; wealth concentration; representation concerns
- **Climate & Environment**: Policy responses to climate change; balancing environment and economy
- **Migration & Borders**: Immigration policy, refugee crises, border security; human rights and sovereignty
- **Conflict & Peace**: Wars, terrorism, peacekeeping; conflict resolution and reconciliation
- **Technology & Politics**: Social media impact, digital disinformation, surveillance, cybersecurity
- **Pandemic Governance**: COVID-19 responses; balance between health and liberty; international cooperation

### Skills & Resources
- **Essential Skills**: Critical thinking, analysis, writing, research, understanding multiple perspectives, patience
- **Learning Paths**: Political science degrees (BA/MA/PhD), law school, policy schools, journalism, internships in government
- **Tools**: Databases (JSTOR, ProQuest), statistical software, news literacy, primary source analysis
- **Career Prep**: Understand current issues deeply; public speaking; internships and networking; writing portfolio

### Fields of Work
- **Political Scientist**: Research political systems, behavior, institutions; teach and publish; universities, think tanks
- **Policy Analyst**: Evaluate and recommend public policies; research impacts; government agencies, think tanks, NGOs
- **Diplomat**: Represent country in international relations; negotiate treaties; foreign service
- **Campaign Manager**: Organize political campaigns; strategy, messaging, volunteer coordination
- **Lobbyist**: Advocate for interests to lawmakers; represent corporations, groups, or causes
- **Political Consultant**: Advise candidates and organizations; campaign strategy, messaging, polling
- **Intelligence Analyst**: Assess geopolitical threats and opportunities; government agencies, private firms
- **Government Official**: Work in public administration; policy implementation, management, public service
- **Journalist/Commentator**: Cover politics, analyze events; newspapers, broadcast, online media
- **Lawyer**: Practice law with political implications; constitutional law, civil rights, government relations
- **NGO Director**: Lead non-governmental organizations; advocacy, international development, human rights
