# Physics

Study the fundamental laws of nature, motion, energy, and the universe - from subatomic particles to galaxies.

## Knowledge Base

### Core Concepts
- **Classical Mechanics**: Motion, forces, Newton's three laws, momentum and energy conservation, work and power, rotational motion
- **Thermodynamics**: Heat and temperature, laws of thermodynamics (entropy, energy conservation), phase transitions, statistical mechanics
- **Electromagnetism**: Electric and magnetic fields, electromagnetic force, Maxwell's equations unifying electricity and magnetism, light as electromagnetic waves
- **Quantum Mechanics**: Wave-particle duality (photons, electrons), uncertainty principle (Heisenberg), atomic structure, quantum tunneling, superposition
- **Relativity**: Special relativity (E=mc², time dilation, length contraction), general relativity (gravity as spacetime curvature), black holes
- **Optics**: Light properties (reflection, refraction, diffraction, interference), lenses and mirrors, color and spectrum, quantum optics

### Major Physicists & Breakthroughs
- **Isaac Newton**: Laws of motion and gravity; classical mechanics foundation
- **Michael Faraday**: Electromagnetic induction; transformer and motor principles
- **James Clerk Maxwell**: Unified electricity and magnetism; light is electromagnetic
- **Albert Einstein**: Special and general relativity; revolutionized understanding of space, time, and gravity
- **Niels Bohr**: Atomic model; quantum mechanics pioneer
- **Werner Heisenberg**: Uncertainty principle; quantum mechanics interpretation
- **Richard Feynman**: Quantum electrodynamics; accessible physics communication
- **Stephen Hawking**: Black holes, quantum gravity, cosmology

### Modern Physics Areas
- **Particle Physics**: Fundamental particles (quarks, leptons), Standard Model, particle accelerators, Higgs boson
- **Cosmology**: Big Bang, universe expansion, dark matter, dark energy, structure of the universe
- **Astrophysics**: Stars (formation, evolution, death), galaxies, black holes, neutron stars, gravitational waves
- **Condensed Matter**: Properties of solids and liquids, semiconductors, superconductivity, quantum materials
- **Nuclear Physics**: Radioactivity, nuclear reactions, nuclear energy, fission and fusion

### Fundamental Constants & Units
- Speed of light (c) = 3×10⁸ m/s
- Planck's constant (h) connects energy and frequency
- Gravitational constant (G) governs gravity
- Avogadro's number relates atoms to macroscopic quantities

### Skills & Resources
- **Essential Skills**: Mathematical thinking, visualization of complex concepts, experimental design, problem-solving
- **Learning Paths**: Physics courses (classical to modern), labs and experiments, internships at observatories or research institutes
- **Tools**: Telescopes, particle detectors, spectrometers, computational simulations, mathematical software
- **Career Prep**: Strong mathematics foundation (calculus, linear algebra), programming skills valuable

### Fields of Work
- **Research Physicist**: Advance theoretical or experimental physics; universities, national labs, research institutes
- **Astrophysicist**: Study stars, galaxies, cosmology; observatories, planetariums, space agencies
- **Nuclear Physicist**: Research atomic nuclei, nuclear energy, medical applications; national labs, hospitals
- **Optical Engineer**: Design lenses, cameras, lasers, fiber optics; aerospace, telecommunications
- **Geophysicist**: Study Earth's physical properties, earthquakes, interior structure; oil/gas, environmental monitoring
- **Medical Physicist**: Apply physics to healthcare, radiation therapy, imaging; hospitals, cancer centers
- **Aerospace Engineer**: Design aircraft, spacecraft, propulsion systems; aerospace companies, space agencies
- **Plasma Physicist**: Study ionized gases; fusion energy research, space physics
